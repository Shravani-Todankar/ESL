How is a Tech Innovation Lab different from a STEM Lab?

Key Area Traditional STEM Lab ENpower Tech Innovation Lab
Core Focus Experimenting with scientific concepts (physics, chemistry, biology) Solving real-world problems using interdisciplinary STEM skills
Learning Approach Theory-driven and curriculum-aligned experiments Inquiry-based, project-driven, and student-led learning
Student Role Follow predefined procedures and instructions Identify problems, research, brainstorm, and innovate solutions
Technology Use Basic equipment like microscopes, beakers, and sensors Advanced tools: 3D printers, robotics kits, coding platforms, IoT, AI/ML
Problem Identification Problems are provided by teachers or textbooks Students identify challenges in their communities or globally
Project Type Short-term lab experiments or demonstrations Long-term, iterative projects with tangible outcomes
Real-World Context Limited focus on real-world applicability Direct connection to SDGs, industry trends, and societal needs
Design Thinking Minimal emphasis on design and iteration Integrated design thinking process (empathize, ideate, prototype, test)
Entrepreneurial Mindset Not a core component Encouraged through innovation challenges, pitching ideas, and business planning
Prototyping Rare or non-existent Central to the learning process with multiple iterations
Collaboration Teacher-led and individual-focused Teamwork and peer learning with diverse skill sets
